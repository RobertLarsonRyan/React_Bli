Card Games Vocabulary Exercises

Below you will find listed all the different exercises that you can do to learn or improve your playing card game vocabulary on Blair English.

For each exercise there is a description of what it is about and what level of English you need to do it (from 'lower-intermediate' to 'advanced').

To do or look at an exercise, simply click on the box or the title of the exercise.


  • Names/Vocabulary Of Playing Cards
    Level: Lower-Intermediate
    Introduces the names/vocabulary of playing cards in English.

  • Types of Poker Hands Part 1
    Level: Intermediate
    This first of two exercises introduces all the names of the different hands/sets of cards that players can have in poker and explains which are the best and the worst hands to have.

  • Types of Poker Hands Part 2
    Level: Intermediate
    This second of two exercises on poker hands tests your ability to rank/order 12 set of cards by their strength (from the strongest to the weakest).

  • English Vocabulary for Poker Part 1
    Level: Intermediate
    This first of two exercises focuses on and explains some of the basic/essential vocabulary used in poker when playing in English.

  • English Vocabulary for Poker Part 2
    Level: Intermediate
    This second of two exercises focuses on and explains the vocabulary used when actually playing in a game of poker in English against other players.

  • English Vocabulary for Texas Holdem Poker Part 1
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    This first of two exercises on Texas Hold'em poker looks at and explains the essential English vocabulary and rules of the game.

  • English Vocabulary for Texas Holdem Poker Part 2
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    This second of two exercises on Texas Hold'em poker looks at and explains the vocabulary used when actually playing in a game of Texas Hold'em.

  • English Vocabulary for Blackjack
    Level: Upper-Intermediate.
    This online exercise both looks at and explains the vocabulary used in the card game blackjack.
