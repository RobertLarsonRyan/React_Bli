Numbers and Figures English Vocabulary Exercises

Below you will find listed all the different exercises that you can do to learn or improve your use of vocabulary when talking about numbers/figures, statistics and charts on Blair English.

For each exercise there is a description of what it is about and what level of English you need to do it (from 'lower-intermediate' to 'advanced').

To do or look at an exercise, simply click on the box or the title of the exercise.


  • Vocabulary for Describing Trends Part 1
    Level: Intermediate
    Introduces alternative ways of describing/saying increases and decreases of numbers and figures.

  • Vocabulary for Describing Trends Part 2
    Level: Intermediate
    Use of different adjectives and adverbs to describe how much numbers and figures increase or decrease.

  • Vocabulary for Describing Trends Part 3
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    This exercise introduces more English vocabulary for describing trends and changes in data on charts/graphs and in tables.

  • Data Table Vocabulary
    Level: Intermediate
    The names in English of the different parts of tables that are used to present data and information.

  • Chart/Line Graph Vocabulary
    Level: Intermediate
    The names in English of the different parts of line graphs and charts (it also contains vocabulary that is used with bar charts).

  • Vocabulary for Explaining & Comparing Chart Data/Trends
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    This exercise introduces English vocabulary for explaining and comparing data and trends on charts/graphs.

  • How to Say Maths Calculations in English
    Level: Lower-Intermediate
    Introduces the English vocabulary for basic calculations and maths.
