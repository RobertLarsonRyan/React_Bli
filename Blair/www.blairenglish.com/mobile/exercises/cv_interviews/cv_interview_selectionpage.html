CV/Resume and Job Interview Exercises

Below you will find listed all the different exercises that you can do on creating CVs/resumes, doing job interviews and the English vocabulary which is used in both of them on Blair English.

For each exercise there is a description of what it is about and what level of English you need to do it (from 'lower-intermediate' to 'advanced').

To do or look at an exercise, simply click on the box or the title of the exercise.


  • Job Interview Questions Practise Test
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Practise answering commonly asked and difficult job interview questions in this online interactive test.

  • Writing a Good CV/Resume
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Online exercise (with example) that looks at both what makes a good CV/resume and how to write one.

  • How to Write a Job Covering Letter/Email
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Focuses on the English phrases and vocabulary that are used on covering letters/emails when applying for a job.

  • How to Describe Yourself in a Job Interview Part 1
    Level: Intermediate
    Focuses on professional adjectives and nouns that can be used to describe yourself on a CV and in an interview.

  • How to Describe Yourself in a Job Interview Part 2
    Level: Intermediate
    More professional adjectives and nouns that can be used to describe yourself on a CV and in an interview.

  • Vocabulary to Use in a CV, Resume & Job Interview Part 1
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Introduces verbs/phrases which make a CV, Resume or Interview sound more professional.

  • Vocabulary to Use in a CV, Resume & Job Interview Part 2
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Introducing more verbs which make a CV, Resume or Interview sound more professional.

  • Vocabulary to Use in a CV, Resume & Job Interview Part 3
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Introducing more verbs/phrases which make a CV, Resume or Interview sound more professional.

  • Job Interview Questions: Why Do You Want To Work For Us?
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Introduces vocabulary for answering this question and 'What makes you suitable for this position?' in an interview.

  • Job Interview Questions: How Do You Deal With Problems?
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Introduces vocabulary for answering questions in an interview on how you have dealt with problems at work.

  • Job Interview Questions: Why Do You Want To Leave Your Current Job?
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Vocabulary and examples for answering the question 'Why do you want to leave your current job?'.

  • How to Answer 'Why Do You Want to Leave Your Current Job?' in a Job Interview
    Level: Advanced
    Understand what you need to think about and do to successfully answer the question 'Why do you want to leave your current job?'.

  • Job Interview Questions: Why Did You Leave This Job?
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Vocabulary and examples for answering a question about why you left a previous job.

  • Job Interview Questions: Answering Hypothetical Questions
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Learn how to both prepare for and answer hypothetical questions asked in a job interviews.

  • Job Interview Questions: Where Do You See Yourself in the Future?
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    Learn how to effectively answer questions in job interviews where they ask you about your plans for the future.

  • Describing Work & Business Processes Part 1
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    This first of two exercises looks at and explains the names of commonly used processes for describing what a person does at work and the different tasks performed in a company.

  • Describing Work & Business Processes Part 2
    Level: Upper-Intermediate
    This seconds of two exercises looks at and explains more commonly used processes for describing what a person does at work and the different tasks performed in a company.

  • Vocabulary to Describe Educational Background for Job Interviews
    Level: Intermediate
    Focuses on vocabulary for describing educational background.

  • Vocabulary for Describing Work Experience for Job Interviews
    Level: Intermediate
    Focuses on vocabulary for describing past and current work experience, with power adjectives.

  • Job Salary & Conditions Vocabulary for Job Interviews Part 1
    Level: Intermediate
    This first of two exercises focuses on the English Vocabulary that is used in job interviews when talking about salaries/money.

  • Job Salary & Conditions Vocabulary for Job Interviews Part 2
    Level: Intermediate
    This second of two exercises focuses on the English Vocabulary that is used in job interviews when talking about the benefits and conditions of a job.
